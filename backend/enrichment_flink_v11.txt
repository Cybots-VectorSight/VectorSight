LEARNED PATTERNS:
  1. Low fill percentage with many elements means stroke-based or outline-only rendering. The shapes exist as boundaries, not as filled regions. The visual result may appear solid to a viewer but the SVG construction uses outlines. Don't confuse SVG rendering technique with visual appearance.
  2. High bilateral symmetry with few elements indicates intentional geometric construction — likely a designed symbol, icon, or simple illustration. The symmetry axis and paired elements define the compositional structure directly.
  3. Concentric groups (elements sharing a center) are multi-ring constructions — a detail feature built from layered shapes. Their position relative to the largest containing element and their size tier determine their structural role.
  4. Read the positive space grid as a low-resolution thumbnail. Trace the boundary between X and dot — that is the silhouette outline. Curved non-rectangular boundaries indicate non-geometric construction. The grid shape is often more informative than individual element properties.
  5. Elements at 0px distance are touching or overlapping — they form continuous visual surfaces. This construction uses stacked shapes for shading, gradient simulation, or color regions rather than discrete separate parts. Read the stacking tree to see layering order.
